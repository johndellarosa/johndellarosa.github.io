Lipids

What would a lipid bilayer be if not for lipids?

Phospholipids

The base of a phospholipid is glycerol, which is a three carbon molecule with a hydroxyl group attached to each carbon. In a phospholipid, 2 of those hydroxyls are attached to fatty acids via ester linkage. The third hydroxyl is connected to a phosphate group. This gives the phospholipid both a hydrophobic and hydrophilic regions.

There are many different variants of phospholipids due to the possible combinations of fatty acids as well as modifications to the phosphate group.

Glycolipid

Sugar molecules attached to lipids. This could be a direct attachment to a lipid or something connected to a lipid, such as glycerol. These are largely found on the extracellular surface of the bilayer and serve in cell-to-cell recognition.

Cholesterol

Four-ring hydrophobic molecule that serves to modulate membrane fluidity and stability.
